Human-centric & Data-driven

Decision Models / Risk Analysis / Network Resilience

Born and raised in Iran, I have developed a passion for employing mathematical tools and engineering insights to solve decision-making problems in the built environment. Im particular, I extensively use machine learning methods and Bayesian statistical models.
In my Ph.D. years, I have focused on modeling the decentralized nature of the decision-making process across real-world interdependent networks such as civil infrastructure system. To this end, I have employed discrete optimization techniques, hierarchical statistical models, and game-theoretic concepts such as auctions and Bayesian games.
Before landing in Rice, I earned a bachelor’s degree in Civil Engineering and a master’s degree in Earthquake Engineering from Sharif University of Technology. My master’s thesis is on prioritization of buildings for retrofit by minimizing the risk to the entire portfolio.
