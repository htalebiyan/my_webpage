Human-centric Decision Models

for Network Resilience

Hesam Talebiyan
Ph.D. Candidate, Rice University

Born and raised in Iran, I have developed a passion for employing mathematical tools and engineering insights to solve decision-making problems in the built environment.

In my Ph.D. years, I have focused on modeling the decentralized nature of the decision-making process across real-world interdependent networks such as civil infrastructure system. To this end, I have employed discrete optimization techniques, hierarchical statistical models, and game-theoretic concepts such as auctions and Bayesian games.

Before landing in Rice, I earned a bachelor’s degree in Civil Engineering and a master’s degree in Earthquake Engineering from Sharif University of Technology. My master’s thesis is on risk-based prioritization of buildings for retrofit.

I am an amateur photographer, avid Persian music listener, and lazy soccer player.
